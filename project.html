Research Question: Does a more favourable business climate lead to higher social mobility?

Find the relevant Colab notebook for this chart here. Social Mobility Index is scraped from the Business Insider website (see the Colab notebook here), and poverty data is scraped from Wikipedia (see the Colab notebook here).

The regression equation was calculated using a regplot Seaborn function in Colab and then plugged into Vega Lite to generate the regression plot. Find the relevant Colab notebook here. R squared (R^2) is calculated in Vega Lite, the reg. R^2 is the square of the correlation between the independent and dependent variables, it measures the proportion of variation in the dependent variable that can be attributed to the independent variable and takes values from 0 to 1. R^2 of 0.72 means that 72% of variation in the Social Mobility Index is explained by Global Entrepreneurship Index, which suggests a strong correlation between the two indicators.
